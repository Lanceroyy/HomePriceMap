Frequently asked questions about Vermont home prices

Which county in Vermont has the most expensive homes?

Chittenden County has the highest median home value in Vermont at $520,092. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Vermont — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

What is the cheapest county in Vermont?

Essex County has the lowest median home value of the 14 Vermont counties tracked here, at $239,437. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do Vermont home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in Vermont has a home value of $375,334, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 58.4% higher. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
