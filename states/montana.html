Frequently asked questions about Montana home prices

Which county in Montana has the most expensive homes?

Madison County has the highest median home value in Montana at $755,719. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Montana — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

What is the cheapest county in Montana?

Prairie County has the lowest median home value of the 56 Montana counties tracked here, at $138,898. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.
