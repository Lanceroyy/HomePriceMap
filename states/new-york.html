Frequently asked questions about New York home prices

Which county in New York has the most expensive homes?

New York County has the highest median home value in New York at $1,217,330. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in New York — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

Which county in New York has the highest household income?

Nassau County has the highest median household income in New York at $146,202. If what you're really after is the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in New York, this is the closer answer — income measures what households actually earn each year, while home values measure what property costs. Neither is quite the same as wealth, which would also account for savings, investments and property people already own outright.

What is the cheapest county in New York?

Allegany County has the lowest median home value of the 62 New York counties tracked here, at $144,740. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do New York home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in New York has a home value of $259,340, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 9.4% higher. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
