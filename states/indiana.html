Frequently asked questions about Indiana home prices

Which county in Indiana has the most expensive homes?

Hamilton County has the highest median home value in Indiana at $471,244. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Indiana — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

Which county in Indiana has the highest household income?

Hamilton County has the highest median household income in Indiana at $121,530. If what you're really after is the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Indiana, this is the closer answer — income measures what households actually earn each year, while home values measure what property costs. Neither is quite the same as wealth, which would also account for savings, investments and property people already own outright.

What is the cheapest county in Indiana?

Vermillion County has the lowest median home value of the 92 Indiana counties tracked here, at $137,921. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do Indiana home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in Indiana has a home value of $236,576, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 0.2% lower. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
