Frequently asked questions about Missouri home prices

Which county in Missouri has the most expensive homes?

Platte County has the highest median home value in Missouri at $396,282. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Missouri — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

Which county in Missouri has the highest household income?

Saint Charles County has the highest median household income in Missouri at $104,692. If what you're really after is the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Missouri, this is the closer answer — income measures what households actually earn each year, while home values measure what property costs. Neither is quite the same as wealth, which would also account for savings, investments and property people already own outright.

What is the cheapest county in Missouri?

Pemiscot County has the lowest median home value of the 113 Missouri counties tracked here, at $92,033. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do Missouri home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in Missouri has a home value of $228,277, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 3.7% lower. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
