Frequently asked questions about Minnesota home prices

Which county in Minnesota has the most expensive homes?

Carver County has the highest median home value in Minnesota at $496,298. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Minnesota — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

What is the cheapest county in Minnesota?

Kittson County has the lowest median home value of the 87 Minnesota counties tracked here, at $152,138. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do Minnesota home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in Minnesota has a home value of $277,455, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 17.1% higher. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
