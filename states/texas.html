Frequently asked questions about Texas home prices

Which county in Texas has the most expensive homes?

Kendall County has the highest median home value in Texas at $593,419. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Texas — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

Which county in Texas has the highest household income?

Rockwall County has the highest median household income in Texas at $127,981. If what you're really after is the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in Texas, this is the closer answer — income measures what households actually earn each year, while home values measure what property costs. Neither is quite the same as wealth, which would also account for savings, investments and property people already own outright.

What is the cheapest county in Texas?

Cochran County has the lowest median home value of the 243 Texas counties tracked here, at $57,837. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do Texas home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in Texas has a home value of $213,544, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 9.9% lower. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
