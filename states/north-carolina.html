Frequently asked questions about North Carolina home prices

Which county in North Carolina has the most expensive homes?

Dare County has the highest median home value in North Carolina at $608,797. This is often searched as the "richest" or "wealthiest" county in North Carolina — though it's worth being precise about what's being measured here: median home value reflects property prices, not household income or net worth. The two usually correlate, but a county can have expensive homes without having the highest-earning residents, particularly in areas with a lot of vacation or second homes.

What is the cheapest county in North Carolina?

Bertie County has the lowest median home value of the 100 North Carolina counties tracked here, at $62,232. Keep in mind that low home prices often reflect limited local job markets, distance from major metro areas, or shrinking population — affordability alone doesn't tell you whether somewhere is a good fit.

How do North Carolina home prices compare to the rest of the country?

The typical (median) county in North Carolina has a home value of $276,714, against $236,981 for the median U.S. county — roughly 16.8% higher. Figures come from Zillow's Home Value Index and are refreshed daily on this site.
